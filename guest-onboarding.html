The Show

About the Podcast

The Bhagavata Podcast is an academic series produced by the Gaudiya Studies Research Programme at the Oxford Centre for Hindu Studies. Each episode is a conversation exploring a chapter of the Bhagavata Purana — its philosophy, history, commentarial tradition, and living relevance.

Our audience spans scholars, practitioners, and curious minds worldwide. The format is unhurried and substantive: two people in genuine dialogue with one of the world's most remarkable texts.

Technical Setup

Recording with Riverside

We record using Riverside.fm, which captures broadcast-quality audio and video from each participant's own device. You don't need an account — Gopinath will send you a direct link to join the session.

How to Join

  • Click the link in your invitation email
  • Open it in Google Chrome — other browsers may not work
  • Allow microphone and camera access when prompted
  • You'll land directly in the studio — no download required

Tech Requirements

  • Microphone: A USB or XLR mic is ideal; built-in laptop mic is a fine fallback — please avoid phone earbuds if possible
  • Camera: Built-in webcam is perfectly fine; external camera is a bonus
  • Internet: Strong WiFi is ideal
  • Environment: A quiet room, away from open windows or air conditioning
  • Lighting: Face a window or lamp — avoid strong light from behind you

Pre-Call Checklist

  • Test your mic and camera in Chrome before the session
  • Close other applications and browser tabs
  • Use headphones or earbuds to avoid echo
  • Silence your phone and notifications during the recording
Preparation

Preparing for the Conversation

What to Bring

  • You don't need anything in particular — guests have found it useful to have notes or texts open nearby
  • Your primary texts, commentaries, or references — feel free to have them open in front of you
  • A central question or tension in your area of focus that you'd like to explore

What to Expect

  • Conversations aim for 45–55 minutes
  • The tone is scholarly but accessible — think a rich dialogue between two friends, not a lecture
  • Bhrigupada will guide the conversation; feel free to push back, go deep, and ask your own questions
  • We lightly edit for clarity but preserve the natural flow of dialogue
Structure

Usual Flow of an Episode

1–2 min
Bhrigupada introduces the chapter and the guest
3–5 min
Guest shares an overview of the chapter — what listeners will encounter, what the text holds
~40 min
Bhrigupada opens with the first question, and the conversation unfolds from there
5 min
Closing reflections — a chance for either guest or host to share something they'd like listeners to carry with them
What to Wear

Dress for the occasion

There's no strict dress code — we want you to feel comfortable and like yourself on camera.

That said, the Bhagavata Podcast sits at the intersection of scholarship and practice, and we love it when that comes through visually too. If you wear tilak, please do — it's a small but meaningful touch that reflects who we are as a community of practitioners. Beyond that, a kurta or saari is always welcome, though professional or business casual works just as well. Whatever helps you show up as both the scholar and the practitioner you are.

In short: be yourself, dress with some care, and don't overthink it. 🙏
